** Test Plumbing

We checked the fluidic tubing and realised that one of the tubes might have some
sort of internal blockage.

The process followed was:
- force air through the tube with a syringe to ensure there are no droplets of water in the tubing
- connect one end of tubing to a balloon full of Nitrogen (N_{2})
- dip the other end in a small "pool" of water
- Watch for bubble as you gently apply pressure on the balloon


With the process above, one of the tubing had effervescent bubbling â€” the other
had no bubbling whatsoever, despite applying considerable pressure on the
balloon.

The conclusion thus, was that we might need more tubing and other plumbing parts
for replacements. We also need to test each of the tubes separately to ensure
that all tubing that we use is clear of blockage/kinks.
